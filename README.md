## **Employee_Performance_Analysis**


## **Project Overview**

Employee Performance Analysis is an end-to-end HR analytics project designed to transform raw employee data into actionable business insights. The project leverages SQL for data analysis and Power BI for interactive dashboard development, enabling HR teams and business leaders to monitor employee performance, engagement, training effectiveness, and workforce distribution.

The project focuses on identifying performance trends, evaluating employee engagement, measuring training outcomes, and supporting data-driven workforce decisions through dynamic visualizations and business-oriented SQL analysis.

## **Objectives**

- Build a structured HR analytics database using SQL.
- Analyze employee performance across departments and job roles.
- Evaluate the relationship between employee engagement and performance.
- Assess the effectiveness of training programs.
- Monitor workforce distribution and demographic insights.
- Develop an interactive Power BI dashboard for HR decision-making.
- Generate actionable business recommendations to improve employee performance and organizational effectiveness.

  ## **Business Problem**

Q.1 Which departments have the highest average employee performance?

Q.2 Rank employees within each department based on performance rating.

Q.3 Which training programs produce the highest employee performance ratings?

Q.4 Does employee engagement influence performance ratings?

Q.5. What is the attrition rate by job role and department?

Q.6. Which long-tenured employees have maintained consistently high performance ratings?

## **Key Business Insights**

- Production department has the largest workforce.
- Employee engagement varies across departments, with Executive Office showing the highest engagement score.
- Most employees fall under the Fully Meets performance category.
- Training participation is relatively balanced across different programs.
- Applicant distribution is fairly even across education levels.
- Salary generally increases with experience, although variation exists across employee groups.

 ## **Business Recommendations**

1. Recognize and reward consistently high-performing employees through structured incentive and recognition programs.
2. Improve employee engagement in departments with lower engagement scores by introducing targeted development initiatives.
3. Evaluate the effectiveness of training programs and invest more in programs associated with higher employee performance.
4. Develop retention strategies for departments exhibiting higher attrition rates.
5. Create personalized learning paths based on employee performance and engagement levels.
6. Use workforce analytics to support succession planning and identify future leadership candidates.
7. Monitor workforce demographics regularly to ensure balanced staffing across departments and job roles.
